### Masters Added
None. All significant masters were already present in the dataset.

### Transmissions Added
- **Tongan Daopi -> Tongan Guanzhi** (primary): Traditional Caodong lineage connection that was missing. This fills a gap in the Yunju -> Tongan Daopi -> Tongan Guanzhi -> Liangshan -> Dayang chain.

### Transmissions Corrected
- **Dayang Jingxuan -> Fushan Fayuan**: Changed type from "primary" to "dharma" and is_primary from true to false. This was a cross-lineage trust arrangement, not a standard dharma transmission. Fushan was a Linji master who held Dayang's Caodong dharma in trust for a future student.
- **Changshui Zixuan**: Changed teacher from Fushan Fayuan to Langye Huijue. Changshui was a Linji lineage student of Langye Huijue, not a student of Fushan.

### Transmissions Removed
- **Dongshan Liangjie -> Mahasattva Fu**: Anachronistic -- Mahasattva Fu (497-569) lived centuries before Dongshan (807-869). This was clearly an error.
- **Xiangyan Zhixian -> Baofeng Weizhao**: Temporally impossible -- Xiangyan died ~898 and Baofeng was born ~1084.

### Dates Corrected
- **Xuedou Zhijian**: Changed from (?-972) to (1105-1192). The previous death year of 972 was impossibly early given his student Tiantong Rujing was born in 1163.
- **Baoci Xingyan**: Removed incorrect death year of 892 (impossible since teacher Fayan Wenyi was born in 885). Set to unknown.
- **Baofeng Weizhao**: Changed from (?-1129) to (1084-~1128) based on Song dynasty historical records.

## Flagged for Human Review

### CRITICAL: Fayan School Gap (Now Resolved)
The Fayan school -- one of the Five Houses of Chan -- had **zero masters assigned** and **no school definition** in the dataset. This has been resolved by:
1. Adding a complete Fayan SchoolDefinition to school-taxonomy.ts
2. Reassigning 6 masters to the Fayan school (Fayan Wenyi, Tiantai Deshao, Yongming Yanshou, Guizong Cezhen, Chongshou Qichou, Baoci Xingyan)
3. Adding school override rules

**Remaining Fayan concern**: The Fayan school had significantly more masters historically. Consider adding: Wenxi Qingliao, Baoen Xuanze's Fayan-connected students, and other figures from the Fayan school's presence at the Wuyue court. The current 6 masters is a minimum viable representation.

### Fushan Fayuan's School Assignment
Fushan Fayuan (991-1067) is currently assigned to Caodong, but he was fundamentally a Linji lineage master who held the Caodong dharma in trust. His own dharma transmission came from Shexian Guisheng in the Linji lineage. The current assignment to Caodong is defensible (his historical role was as the bridge for the Caodong revival), but a case could be made for Linji. A human decision is needed.

### Baofeng Weizhao -- Orphan Master
After removing the incorrect transmission from Xiangyan Zhixian, Baofeng Weizhao is now an orphan (no incoming transmissions). He was a Song dynasty Caodong master (1084-~1128) but his specific dharma lineage needs to be identified. He may have studied under Furong Daokai or one of his successors.

### Baoci Xingyan -- Dates Unknown
After removing the impossible death year of 892, Baoci Xingyan has no dates. Sources confirm him as Fayan Wenyi's student, but his life dates are uncertain. He likely lived in the mid-to-late 10th century.

### Yangshan Huiji Dates Disputed
Two sets of dates appear in scholarly sources: 807-883 and 813-890. The dataset uses 807-883 (matching Terebess and traditional Chinese sources). The 813-890 dates appear in some Western encyclopedias. Both are defensible.

### Tongan Daopi -> Tongan Guanzhi Lineage
The standard lineage chart places Tongan Guanzhi as Tongan Daopi's student. However, the earliest source (Transmission of the Lamp, c. 1004) lists Guanzhi as a student of Tongan Wei (a different monk, student of Jufeng Puman). The Daopi -> Guanzhi connection first appears in Huihong's Chanlin sengbao zhuan (1119). I have added the traditional connection but flagged this scholarly dispute.

### Caodong Lineage -- Hongzhi and Tiantong Zongjue
Tiantong Zongjue succeeded Hongzhi Zhengjue as abbot of Tiantong Monastery but was NOT Hongzhi's dharma heir. Zongjue received dharma transmission from Zhenxie Qingliao (via Changlu Qingliao <- Danxia Zichun <- Furong Daokai). The current data correctly shows this relationship. Note that the path from Furong Daokai to Tiantong Rujing goes through two parallel branches that converge.

### Potential Missing Figures
The following historically significant figures may be worth adding in a future pass:
- **Chuanzi Decheng's "Boat Monk" episode partners** (already present as Chuanzi himself)

- **Wansong Xingxiu** (1166-1246, compiler of the Book of Serenity, Caodong)

The Caodong school has the most complete representation, which is appropriate given its historical longevity and continuing existence as Soto Zen. The Yunmen and Guiyang schools, which did not survive as independent institutions, have proportionally fewer masters, reflecting both the historical record and the school's absorption into the Linji tradition.

## Cross-Tradition Observations

1. **The Qingyuan line as container**: The Qingyuan line school currently serves as a "catch-all" for pre-Five-Houses masters in the Shitou lineage. This is appropriate but creates a heterogeneous group. Figures like Shitou, Yaoshan, Deshan, and Xuefeng are in this category because they preceded the formal school divisions.

2. **Nanyue line symmetry**: Similarly, the Nanyue line contains many Mazu-lineage masters who predate the formal Linji/Guiyang division. The corrections in this audit moved ~14 masters from wrong schools to Nanyue line.

3. **Fushan Fayuan as bridge**: The Caodong revival through Fushan Fayuan is one of the most unusual events in Chan history. The data now correctly marks the Dayang -> Fushan transmission as type "dharma" (not "primary"), reflecting its extraordinary nature.

4. **School boundary ambiguity**: Several masters studied with teachers from multiple lineages. Longya Judun studied with both Dongshan (Caodong) and Cuiwei Wuxue (Qingyuan line). Such figures are assigned to their primary dharma heir lineage but the boundary is inherently fuzzy.

5. **The Five Houses completeness**: With the addition of the Fayan school definition, all Five Houses of Chan (Caodong, Linji, Yunmen, Guiyang, Fayan) now have proper school definitions and at least some masters assigned. This was a critical gap that has been resolved.
