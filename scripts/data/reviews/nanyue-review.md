## Flagged for Human Review

1. **Xuan Huaichang's teacher**: Added as a Linji/Huanglong master without a teacher transmission because his specific predecessor in the Huanglong lineage could not be confidently identified in the existing database. Research indicates he was the eighth-generation Huanglong teacher, but the intermediate masters between Huanglong Huinan (gen 1) and Xuan Huaichang (gen 8) are not all in the database.

2. **Huanglong Huinan -> Myoan Eisai dharma link**: There is an existing "dharma" type transmission from Huanglong Huinan to Myoan Eisai (72-year temporal gap). This represents a lineage claim rather than personal transmission. The correct personal transmission (Xuan Huaichang -> Eisai) has now been added. The dharma link could be retained as a lineage marker or removed.

3. **Guishan Daan -> Dasui Fazhen temporal overlap**: Only 5 years overlap (Daan d. 883, Dasui b. 878). This is a validation warning but the transmission is historically attested in the Transmission of the Lamp.

4. **Zhongfeng Mingben -> Huanyou Zhengchuan**: Pre-existing 226-year temporal gap in this transmission (outside our scope but notable since both are Yangqi line). This likely represents a lineage claim rather than direct personal transmission.

5. **Mazu -> Korean Seon masters**: Several temporal warnings for Mazu's transmissions to Korean Seon figures (Hyeonuk, Toyun, Muyeom, Chiseon Doheon). These were added by the Korean reviewer and may represent lineage claims rather than direct transmission.

## School Balance Assessment

After corrections, the school counts are:

- **Nanyue line**: Grew from 7 to 26 masters, now properly including Nanyue Huairang, Mazu Daoyi, Baizhang Huaihai, and Mazu's major disciples who didn't found their own schools. This correctly parallels the Qingyuan line which includes Shitou, Dongshan, etc.
- **Linji**: Adjusted from 52 to 47 masters. Previously inflated by including pre-Linji Nanyue line figures. Now properly starts with Huangbo Xiyun and Linji Yixuan. Gained Xuan Huaichang and Sansheng Huiran.
- **Yangqi line**: Reduced from 18 to 14 masters. Previously included chronologically impossible assignments (Zhangjing Huaiyun d. 880, Guishan Daan d. 883, Dasui Fazhen d. 963).

The rebalancing corrects a systematic miscategorization where figures from the broader Nanyue branch were retroactively assigned to descendant schools that hadn't yet been founded. The corrected scheme matches the pattern already established for the Qingyuan side of the lineage.

## Cross-Tradition Observations

1. **Caodong school had absorbed several Nanyue line figures**: Guizong Zhichang, Damei Fachang, and Luzu Baoyun were all Mazu's direct students incorrectly placed in Caodong. This likely happened because automated categorization couldn't distinguish between "mentioned in Caodong texts" and "member of Caodong school."

2. **Yunmen school similarly absorbed Nanyue figures**: Panshan Baoji and Mayu Baoche, both Mazu students, were incorrectly in Yunmen. Some of these figures appear in Yunmen-lineage koan collections, which may have caused the miscategorization.

3. **The Guiyang school was underrepresented**: Lingyun Zhiqin (a Guishan Lingyou student famous for the peach blossom awakening) was incorrectly in Linji. The Guiyang school may need a dedicated review to find similar miscategorizations.

4. **Eisai's lineage was fundamentally wrong**: The database had Eisai studying under Xuedou Chongxian (Yunmen school, d. 1052), which is chronologically impossible and misrepresents his Huanglong lineage. Eisai actually studied under Xuan Huaichang, a Huanglong branch master, making him part of the Linji/Huanglong line, not Yangqi.
