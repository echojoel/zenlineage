Guishan Da'an (also known as Changqing Da'an, 793–883) is uniformly recorded
as a dharma heir of Baizhang Huaihai. The Terebess master list gives an explicit
designation; the Changqing Da'an page on Terebess preserves a teaching exchange
in which Baizhang instructed him using ox metaphors and Da'an "received this
instruction" and "sought nothing further," indicating transmission. He later
succeeded Guishan Lingyou's abbacy on Dagui Mountain — a position that itself
attests to his standing as a Baizhang-lineage heir.
