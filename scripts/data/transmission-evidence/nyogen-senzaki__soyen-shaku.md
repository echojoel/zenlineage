  Two positions exist in the scholarship:
  (1) FORMAL HEIR: Terebess presents a full Rinzai lineage chart placing Senzaki
      directly as Soyen Shaku's dharma successor in the Inzan line (Hakuin →
      Gasan → Inzan → Taigen → Gisan → Kosen → Soyen Shaku → Senzaki).
  (2) NOT A FORMAL HEIR: The Soyen Shaku Wikipedia article's "Dharma heirs"
      section lists ONLY Tetsuo Sōkatsu (Ryobo-an Sokatsu); Senzaki is absent.
      The Tricycle article is unambiguous: "neither received formal dharma
      transmission." Hollow Bones Zen (hollowboneszen.org) similarly states
      Senzaki was "not a formal dharma heir of Soyen Shaku, who had no successors."
      Senzaki's own account says he "gained nothing" from all teachers except
      Soyen Shaku, suggesting deep influence without confirming formal inka.

  The consensus among scholarly sources leans toward informal/contested status.
  The Terebess lineage chart may reflect a retrospective lineage reconstruction
  rather than a contemporaneous inka document. This mirrors the Deshimaru
  situation — a well-documented teacher-student relationship without standard
  formal certification.
