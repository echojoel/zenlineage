reducer_notes: |
  Tier B: multiple independent hostnames, strong academic coverage. The
  transmission from Daman Hongren (Fifth Patriarch) to Yuquan Shenxiu is one
  of the best-documented teacher-student relationships in early Chan. Shenxiu
  studied under Hongren for roughly six years (c. 651–657), rose to be head
  monk of Hongren's community, and was widely recognized as the "Sixth
  Patriarch" prior to the later polemic by Shenhui that reattributed that
  title to Huineng. The Encyclopedia of Buddhism (institutional), Internet
  Encyclopedia of Philosophy (academic), New World Encyclopedia (reference),
  and two Wikipedia articles (reference) all independently corroborate the
  direct dharma transmission. No source contradicts it; the historical
  controversy concerns the rival claim of Huineng, not the reality of
  Shenxiu's student relationship with Hongren.
