Luzu Baoyun of Chizhou is recorded as a direct dharma heir of Mazu Daoyi on
the Terebess Asia Online page, which states explicitly: "was a disciple of Mazu."
His brotherhood with Nanquan Puyuan under Mazu is also confirmed there. He is
best known for the koan practice of silently facing the wall when students
arrived, featured in Chan collections for generations.
