reducer_notes: |
  Tier D confirmed after additional research. The academic literature (Chen
  Jinhua, Tandfonline 2015) explicitly concludes that identifying the "Damo"
  who mentored Mahasattva Fu with Bodhidharma (Puti Damo) is "hard, if not
  completely impossible" given available textual and epigraphic evidence. The
  Damo of Fu's hagiography likely contributed to the later Bodhidharma image
  through conflation, but is not demonstrably the same historical person.

  Multiple independent sources consistently position Mahasattva Fu and
  Bodhidharma as parallel contemporaries of equal stature (the "Three
  Mahāsattvas of the Liang Dynasty" alongside Baozhi), not as student and
  teacher. Wikipedia states explicitly that "no formal lineage transmission
  from Bodhidharma is documented." Mahasattva Fu's hagiography portrays him
  as a self-claimed reincarnation of Maitreya — a figure who would not
  typically require or claim Chan dharma transmission from a human teacher.

  This edge should be removed from the lineage graph unless a primary
  Chinese hagiographic source (e.g., the Xu Gaoseng Zhuan, Fozu Tongji, or
  Jingde Chuandeng Lu entry for Fu Dashi) can be produced that explicitly
  names his Damo mentor as Bodhidharma. Community / popular tradition is
  not sufficient: the scholarly consensus is that the identification is
  unverifiable and likely anachronistic.
