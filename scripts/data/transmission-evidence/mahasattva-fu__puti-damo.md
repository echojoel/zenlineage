  EDGE UNVERIFIABLE AS MODELED — confirmed D-tier.

  RELATIONSHIP: Mahasattva Fu (傅大士 / Fu Dashi, 497–569) and Bodhidharma / Puti
  Damo (菩提達摩, d. c. 532) are positioned as parallel contemporaries of equal
  standing — the "Three Mahāsattvas of the Liang Dynasty" alongside Baozhi. This
  is a parity relationship, not a teacher-student one.

  SCHOLARLY CONSENSUS: Chen Jinhua (Tandfonline, peer-reviewed, 2015) explicitly
  concludes that identifying the "Damo" mentioned in Fu Dashi's hagiography as
  Bodhidharma is "hard, if not completely impossible" given available textual and
  epigraphic evidence. The Damo figure in Fu's hagiography likely contributed to
  the later Bodhidharma image through conflation, but is not demonstrably the same
  historical person.

  HAGIOGRAPHIC CONTEXT: Fu Dashi's own tradition portrays him as a self-claimed
  reincarnation of Maitreya — a figure of independent cosmic status who would not
  typically require or receive Chan dharma transmission from a human teacher.
  Wikipedia states explicitly that "no formal lineage transmission from Bodhidharma
  is documented."

  CONCLUSION: This edge should be removed from the lineage graph. No primary
  Chinese hagiographic source (Xu Gaoseng Zhuan, Fozu Tongji, or Jingde Chuandeng
  Lu) has been produced that explicitly names Fu Dashi's Damo mentor as the same
  Bodhidharma who is the first Chan patriarch. The scholarly consensus is that the
  identification is unverifiable and likely anachronistic.
