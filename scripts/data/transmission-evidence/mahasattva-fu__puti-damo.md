reducer_notes: |
  The academic literature unambiguously states the teacher-student claim
  cannot be verified. The Tandfonline article (Chen Jinhua, 2015) investigates
  whether the "Damo" who was Mahasattva Fu's mentor is the same as Bodhidharma
  (Puti Damo) and concludes the identification is "hard, if not completely
  impossible" given current textual and epigraphic evidence. Wikipedia on Fu
  Dashi states he is "popularly revered alongside Bodhidharma and Baozhi as
  the Three Mahāsattvas of the Liang Dynasty" — as contemporaries of similar
  stature, not as teacher and student. Mahasattva Fu is typically described as
  a "responsive manifestation" who did not receive formal Chan transmission
  from a human teacher. No source positively identifies Damo in Fu's hagiography
  as Bodhidharma. Tier D maintained. This edge should be removed unless primary
  Chinese source material specifically identifies the Damo mentor as Bodhidharma.
