  EDGE IS WRONG AS MODELED — downgraded to D.

  TEMPORAL GAP: Seosan Hyujeong (西山休靜 / Cheongheo Hyujeong, 1520–1604) and

  direct transmission; there are SIX documented intermediate masters.

  DIRECT TEACHER: Wikipedia (Hyujeong article) confirms Hyujeong's direct dharma
  teacher was Buyong Yeonggwan (芙蓉靈觀, 1485–1571). Hyujeong himself stated:
  "Buyong [Yeonggwan] is my father." The correct direct-transmission edge is
  seosan-hyujeong → buyong-yeonggwan, not seosan-hyujeong → taego-bou.

  FULL CHAIN (Terebess lineage chart):
  Taego Bou (1301–1382) → Hwanam Honsu (1320–1392) → Gugok Gakun →
  Byeokgye Jeongsim (?–1492) → Byeoksong Jieom (1464–1534) →
  Buyong Yeonggwan (1485–1571) → Seosan Hyujeong (1520–1604)

  SCHOLARLY STATUS: Wikipedia (Korean Seon) describes the retrospective claim
  linking Hyujeong to Taego Bou as "flawed" — it was invented in the 17th century
  (after Hyujeong's death) when lineage politics changed. The Brill academic
  journal (2019) confirms these were "retrospective constructions" not reflecting
  contemporaneous transmission understanding.

  CONCLUSION: The seosan-hyujeong→taego-bou edge should be removed from the graph.
  The correct edge is seosan-hyujeong→buyong-yeonggwan (direct) plus the full
  upstream chain through the six intermediaries listed above.
