  TEMPORAL GAP: Vinitaruci (毘尼多流支) fl. 6th century, died c. 594 CE; Trần

  intermediate patriarchs of the Tì-ni-đa-lưu-chi school. This is not a direct
  personal transmission.

  DIRECT TEACHER: Multiple independent sources (Wikipedia Tuệ Trung, Parallax
  Press, Yên Tử Tùng Lâm institutional, Khyentse Foundation institutional) all
  confirm that Trần Nhân Tông's actual direct Zen teacher was Tuệ Trung Thượng Sĩ
  (1230–1291), a lay practitioner in the Vô Ngôn Thông (Wu Yantong) lineage —
  NOT the Vinitaruci lineage. The correct direct-transmission edge is
  tran-nhan-tong → tue-trung.

  CORRECT INTERPRETATION: Trần Nhân Tông is documented as having synthesized and
  unified all three Vietnamese Thiền streams — Tì-ni-đa-lưu-chi (Vinitaruci), Vô
  Ngôn Thông (Wu Yantong), and Thảo Đường — into the new Trúc Lâm school. The
  Vinitaruci connection is therefore a school-synthesis/integration claim, not a
  descent through personal transmission. Yên Tử Tùng Lâm (institutional) and
  tvtuevien.net (sangha) explicitly describe this synthesis framing.

  CONCLUSION: The tran-nhan-tong→vinitaruci edge misrepresents a school-synthesis
