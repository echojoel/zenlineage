  IMPORTANT FLAG — LINEAGE ANCESTRY, NOT DIRECT TRANSMISSION.
  Keizan Jokin (1268–1325) and Keido Chisan (1879–1967) are separated by
  approximately 15 generations and ~600 years. They were never contemporaries.

  Keido Chisan's actual direct dharma teacher was Kohō Hakugan (孤峰白岩),
  at whose hands he was ordained at Eikoji, Hakui, Ishikawa Prefecture (ca. 1889).
  The Terebess Jiyu-Kennett lineage chart explicitly places Kohō Hakugan
  immediately before Keidō Chisan in the successive dharma transmission chain.
  The Keizan Study PDF (Soto Zen Buddhist Association 2010) states this plainly:
  "Yokoji is the temple where Keido Chisan was ordained by Koho Hakugan."

  The full intermediate chain (Terebess): Keizan Jōkin → Meihō Sotetsu →
  … → Manzan Dōhaku → … → Kohō Hakugan → Keidō Chisan.

  Four sources confirm the lineage ancestry connection, two of which are
  institutional (Dharma Rain, sotozen.com). However, none establish a
  DIRECT teacher-student transmission between Keizan Jokin and Keido Chisan.
  Tier remains C: the institutional and academic sources available confirm only
  the ancestry chain, not a direct transmission edge. Tier B/A upgrade is
  structurally blocked by the non-directness of this edge.
