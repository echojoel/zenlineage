reducer_notes: |
  Tier C — UPGRADED. A key academic PDF hosted on Terebess ("Outside of a
  Small Circle: Sōtō Zen Commentaries on Dōgen's Shōbōgenzō") explicitly
  calls Tenkei Denson a "Gesshū disciple" alongside Manzan and Menzan:
  "Manzan, Menzan, and Tenkei, all Gesshū disciples despite severe [...]"
  This is the first direct source found that names Tenkei Denson as a
  disciple of Gesshū Sōko (1618–1696).

  LINEAGE CHANT CAVEAT: Every major Western Sōtō chant lineage (Maezumi,
  Sacramento, Upaya, Dutch Zenmeditation.eu) lists the chain as:
  ... Goho Kainon → Tenkei Denson (1648-1735) → Zozan Monko ...
  These are sequential recitation chains for the Maezumi/Baian Hakujun
  sub-lineage and do NOT necessarily mean Goho Kainon was Tenkei Denson's
  direct dharma teacher. The chants reflect the Maezumi branch's specific
  transmission through one of Tenkei's descendants, skipping Gesshu Soko
  (who was further back in the lineage).

  RECONCILIATION: The Gesshu Soko → Tenkei Denson edge is the dharma
  transmission relationship. The Goho Kainon → Tenkei Denson sequential
  position in chant lineages is a separate branch that runs through the
  Maezumi family abbot succession at Sōjiji, not a claim that Goho Kainon
  was Tenkei's personal dharma teacher.

  Note: Gesshu Soko's documented primary heir is Manzan Dohaku; both the
  Terebess Gesshu Soko page and Wikipedia confirm this. The Terebess PDF
  establishes Tenkei Denson as a second Gesshū disciple. Tier C (one
  academic source, no institutional corroboration yet).
