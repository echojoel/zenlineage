Traditional Chan genealogy consistently records Nanyue Huairang (677–744) as a
principal dharma heir of the Sixth Patriarch Huineng. Multiple sources confirm
this: Wikipedia states he "studied under the sixth patriarch Huineng for eight
years"; the Encyclopedia of Buddhism calls him "the foremost student of Dajian
Huineng." Two of the Five Houses of Chan — Linji and Guiyang — trace through
this edge. Modern scholars (McRae) question historicity, noting the earliest
Platform Sutra lacks Nanyue's name and that an 801 CE text points to Lao'an as
his primary teacher, but all reference sources treat the Nanyue→Huineng edge as
the canonical lineage claim.
