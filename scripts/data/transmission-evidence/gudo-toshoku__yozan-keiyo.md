Gudo Toshoku (1577/79–1661) received inka (dharma transmission) from Yozan Keiyo at Shotaku-in Temple, Myoshinji. This is confirmed by two Wikipedia articles, the JapaneseWiki biographical source, and the Daiyuzenji institutional lineage. Gudo Toshoku became the 14th abbot of Myoshinji and a key link in the chain leading to Shido Bunan, Shoju Rojin, and ultimately Hakuin Ekaku.
