reducer_notes: |
  EDGE IS NOT A PERSONAL TRANSMISSION — downgraded to D.

  TEMPORAL GAP: Dajian Huineng (大鑒慧能, 638–713) died 445 years before Jinul
  (知訥, 1158–1210) was born. This edge cannot represent a direct personal
  dharma transmission.

  NATURE OF RELATIONSHIP: This is a textual/intellectual transmission only. Jinul
  (Bojo Jinul, 普照知訥) had no formal living teacher in Seon. Three independent
  Wikipedia articles confirm this: the Jinul article states he "studied the entire
  Tripiṭaka and had a series of awakenings" through scriptures; the "Wisdom without
  a teacher" article states he "neither received transmission from a recognized Sŏn
  master nor did he ever enter into an extended formal relationship with a teacher."

  JINUL'S OWN WORDS: The academic source (Chun Jung-kwon, 2012, International
  Journal of Buddhist Thought and Culture) records that Jinul himself identified
  the Platform Sutra as "his original teacher." This confirms the textual
  relationship is self-reported by Jinul, but it is precisely the kind of
  metaphorical teacher-claim that cannot be modeled as a lineage transmission edge.

  THREE AWAKENINGS: Jinul's spiritual development came through three textual
  sources — Huineng's Platform Sutra (first awakening, 1182), Li Tongxuan's
  Huayan commentary (second awakening), and Dahui Zonggao's letters (third
  awakening). None involved a living transmitter.

  CONCLUSION: This edge should be removed from the lineage graph or clearly
  annotated as "textual/editorial bridge — not dharma transmission." Including
  it as a standard lineage edge misrepresents Jinul's actual historical position
  as a master who awakened independently through scripture (mushi-dokugo). If a
  "textual transmission" edge type exists in the graph schema, this is the
  canonical example for it.
