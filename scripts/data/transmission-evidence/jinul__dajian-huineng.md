reducer_notes: |
  This is an editorial/textual transmission bridge, not a living teacher-to-student dharma transmission. Jinul (普照知訥, 1158–1210) had no formal living teacher of note; he awakened independently through reading Huineng's Platform Sutra (first awakening, 1182), Li Tongxuan's Huayan commentary (second awakening), and Dahui Zonggao's letters (third awakening). The academic source (IJBTC 2012, Chun Jung-kwon) explicitly records that Jinul identified the Platform Sutra as "his original teacher," while the Wikipedia "Wisdom without a teacher" article provides the most direct statement that he "neither received transmission from a recognized Sŏn master nor did he ever enter into an extended formal relationship with a teacher." The lineage edge thus represents intellectual and spiritual inheritance through text, not personal certification. Three independent sources now document this textual relationship (two Wikipedia pages + one peer-reviewed academic journal), and the academic source confirms Jinul's own identification of Huineng's text as foundational. Tier C retained because no institutional source (e.g. Jogye Order) was retrievable for this specific claim, and the edge itself remains an editorial bridge.
