Zhaozhou Congshen (778–897) is universally confirmed as the principal dharma
heir of Nanquan Puyuan. His Wikipedia article states he "received the Dharma
from him" and "continued to practice under Nanquan until the latter's death."
Nanquan's Wikipedia article calls Zhaozhou "the most famous" of his seventeen
dharma successors. The Encyclopedia of Buddhism corroborates this. The famous
Mu koan and "washing the bowls" exchange trace directly to this lineage.
