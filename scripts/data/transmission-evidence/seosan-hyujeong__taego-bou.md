  Seosan Hyujeong (西山休靜 / Cheongheo Hyujeong, 1520–1604) is separated from Taego Bou (1301–1382) by approximately 150 years and SIX intermediate masters. The Terebess Korean lineage chart (retrieved 2026-05-22) now provides the complete documented chain: Taego Bou → Hwanam Honsu (1320–1392) → Gugok Gakun → Byeokgye Jeongsim (?–1492) → Byeoksong Jieom (1464–1534) → Buyong Yeonggwan (1485–1567/71) → Seosan Hyujeong (1520–1604). This is definitively NOT a direct transmission — it is a 7-generation institutional lineage succession. The Wikipedia Korean Seon article adds critical scholarly context: this retrospective lineage claim was first made in the 17th century (after Seosan's time) and is described as "flawed" because "the assumptions guiding lineage practices in the fourteenth century were no longer guiding lineage practices in the seventeenth century." The edge as currently modeled (direct Taego→Seosan) misrepresents this as a one-hop transmission. The graph should either (a) represent all six intermediate masters as separate edges, or (b) annotate this edge as a multi-generational institutional lineage claim with the full chain documented here.
