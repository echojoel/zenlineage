Lineage Visualizer: Animated Flow Concept

An organic, meditative experience — lineages flow like rivers branching through time

Visual Metaphor

Interaction Model

🔍 Zoom & Pan

Infinite canvas — zoom into any era or region. At high zoom, individual masters appear as glowing nodes on the flow. At low zoom, you see the full sweep of Zen history as branching rivers of light.

⏱️ Time Scrubber

A timeline bar at the bottom. Drag to travel through centuries. Lineages animate into existence — streams appear, branch, thicken, and sometimes fade. Historical events appear as annotations.

👆 Node Interaction

Hover: Glow effect, name tooltip with dates.
Click: Sidebar panel slides in with summary.
Double-click: Navigate to full profile page.
Right-click: "Focus lineage" — highlights just this master's upstream/downstream.

🎨 Visual Language

Each school has a distinct color: Rinzai/Linji, Soto/Caodong, Sanbo, Early Chan, Indian Patriarchs. Stream width = number of active practitioners/temples in that era.

Technology: Canvas Rendering

For smooth animation of hundreds of nodes and flowing paths, I recommend HTML Canvas + WebGL via a library like:

  • PixiJS — 2D WebGL renderer, excellent for smooth animations, particle effects for the "flowing" feel
  • React Flow — purpose-built for node graphs, but may feel too "techy" for this organic aesthetic
  • D3.js + Canvas — maximum control, proven for data visualization, but more manual work
  • Three.js (2D mode) — if we later want 3D depth or parallax effects

Recommendation: D3.js for data/layout + PixiJS for rendering — best balance of data-handling power and visual quality
